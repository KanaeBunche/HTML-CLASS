Book Report: Wonder by R.J. Palacio

Introduction

Wonder tells the story of August "Auggie" Pullman, a boy born with a rare facial difference. After being homeschooled for years, Auggie enters middle school for the first time, facing challenges that test his courage and resilience.

Book Cover

Author's Background

R.J. Palacio is an American author and graphic designer. She wrote Wonder in 2012, inspired by a real-life encounter with a child who had a craniofacial condition. The book has since become a bestseller and has been adapted into a film.

Plot Summary

The story follows Auggie as he navigates middle school, making new friends while dealing with bullying and people's reactions to his appearance. Throughout the book, we see events from the perspectives of Auggie and several supporting characters, showing how kindness, empathy, and understanding transform relationships.

Key Themes

  • Kindness: Small acts of kindness make a big difference.
  • Empathy: Understanding others' feelings helps build connections.
  • Courage: It takes courage to be yourself, especially when others judge you.

Notable Quote

"When given the choice between being right and being kind, choose kind." – Mr. Browne

This quote reflects the book’s core message about prioritizing kindness over winning or being correct.

Character Analysis

  1. Auggie Pullman: Brave and resilient, learning to accept himself.
  2. Via Pullman: Auggie's sister, dealing with her own struggles of living in his shadow.
  3. Jack Will: Auggie's friend, who learns the value of true friendship.
  4. Julian Albans: A bully who struggles to understand kindness and empathy.

Writing Style

Palacio uses a simple, accessible style, switching between multiple characters’ points of view. This approach helps readers understand the complexities of relationships and emotions from different perspectives.

Movie Adaptation

A film adaptation of Wonder was released in 2017, starring Jacob Tremblay as Auggie. It captures the spirit of the book, highlighting the importance of kindness and acceptance.

Conclusion

Wonder teaches us that being different is not a weakness—it’s an opportunity to grow and learn. The book emphasizes the importance of choosing kindness and standing up for others, even when it's difficult.
